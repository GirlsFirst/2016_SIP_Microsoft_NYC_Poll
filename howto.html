Necessities for Voting:

While many states have different voting laws, the vast majority require:


  • The voter to be of the age 18 or older*
  • A completed Voter Registration Form to have been submitted
  • Identification such as a Driver's License (some states do not require Photo ID with the License)

*This is a nationwide law and not based upon state

Tips for Voting Day:


  • Make sure your voter registration was approved!
  • Double check your polling location
  • Check what forms of ID your voting location requires
  • Understand your ballot before voting by looking up your states ballot example
  • Vote carefully
  • You have to be a resident of the United States.
  • You can not be in jail or in probation.
  • If you just moved in, you can always check online to see how long you have to wait in order for you to vote. e.g: You have to be a New York resident for at least 30 days in order for you to vote.
  • You have to be 18 years old before the next election.

What do I need to register to vote as an absentee?

  • Have some sort picture identification. Some states aren’t strict so they’ll accept an I.D without your picture. (Recommended: bring an I.D with your picture for safe measures)
  • You will need to use your first and last name!
  • Include where you are living as of now.
  • If you are a college student you are able to register to vote from where you feel most comfortable, meaning from your apartment, your dorm or even your parents home.
  • If you are setting your address to where your parents live, then send in your absentee ballot as early as possible.
